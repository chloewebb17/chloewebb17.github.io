Mayak Eggs

Prep Time: 20 minutes Marinate Time: 24 hours Serving: 6 eggs

Marinated eggs so good they're called "drug eggs" in Korean!

Instructions

  • 1 cup water

Ingredients

Marinade

  • 1 cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp Lao Gan Ma
    • Special note: A traditional ingredient in mayak eggs are Korean chili peppers. I don't have these, so I use Lao Gan Ma for spice instead.
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp ground ginger

Dry Ingredients

  • 6 soft-boiled eggs
  • 1-3 cloves of garlic (depending on size)
  • 1-2 chopped green onions
  • 1/3 onion, sliced
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • Pinch of sesame seeds
  • Pinch of salt
  • Ice cubes & water

Instructions

  1. Bring water to a rolling boil in a pot (make sure the water height is about 1 inch taller than the height of an egg)
  2. Add a pinch of salt and a tablespoon of vinegar to the water
  3. Gently set eggs in the water using an egg ladle or any other method that will not crack the eggs
  4. Boil the eggs for 6 minutes
  5. Find a large bowl and fill it with cold water and some ice
  6. After 6 minutes, immediately remove the eggs from the water and place into the ice bath
  7. Pour all marinade ingredients into a tupperware or glass container (make sure it is not filled to the top), mix until honey is thoroughly dissolved
  8. Chop onions, garlic, and green onions, and then add it into the marinade
  9. Cover the surface of the liquid in sesame seeds
  10. After the eggs have rested for 10 minutes, carefully peel them under running cold water
  11. Place the eggs in the marinade, seal the container, and place in the fridge overnight
  12. Serve on a bowl of rice and enjoy!
