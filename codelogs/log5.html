Dimensions and Resolution

Dimensions and resolution both refer to the size of an image, but they operate slightly differently. A dimension is the actual, physical size of the image. Most sizes have an immutable real-life measurement, for example, 1900 pixels wide. Resolution is the density of that image on your screen, or rather, how much detail can be observed. Maybe this 1900 pixel image is only being displayed on a 240p monitor, or maybe it's in 1080p. You might recognize those sizes from Youtube video quality settings. You also might notice that older videos only go up to 480p or so. This is because over time, screens have become higher-resolution, and as a result, once high-quality videos now look very old and blurry.
