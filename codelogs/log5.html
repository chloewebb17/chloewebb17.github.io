Dimensions and Resolution

Dimensions and resolution both refer to the size of an image, but they operate slightly differently. A dimension is the actual, physical size of the image. Most sizes have an immutable real-life measurement, for example, 1900 pixels wide. Resolution is the density of that image on your screen, or rather, how much detail can be observed. Maybe this 1900 pixel image is only being displayed on a 240p monitor, or maybe it's in 1080p. You might recognize those sizes from Youtube video quality settings. You also might notice that older videos only go up to 480p or so. This is because over time, screens have become higher-resolution, and as a result, once high-quality videos now look very old and blurry.

Types of image files

  1. GIF

    2. Known for its controversial pronunciation, .gif (or Graphics Interchange Format) files have the very special ability to save multiple frames, aka animations. They are also highly compressed without losing visual quality, which helps save on storage. .gifs have been a staple of websites for decades, but recently, they've become commonplace within chats as well.

  2. PNG

    3. .png (Portable Network Graphics) files are extremely high quality, ideal for artwork and detailed photography, and also support transparency. Transparent parts of images are essentially invisible, so you can have an image with no background.

    Render from Doeyduey on DeviantArt.

  3. JPEG

    4. .jpg (Joint Photographic Experts Group) is the most widely used image file type in the world. JPEG files are extremely small, and ideal when low on storage. The downside there is that the file format is very lossy, and highly compressed images show evidence of "JPEG artifacting," which describes the visual noise that this compression produces.

    This image file is not a .jpg, but the skateboard within it is a heavily artifacted .jpg.

  4. WebP

    5. Poor, misunderstood .webp files. For web development, .webp are a fantastic choice as they were specifically developed to be a low-storage replacement for all three of the files types listed above. They support lossy and lossless image compression, transparency, animation, and store metadata well. However, .webp has a bad reputation because many external computer programs do not support .webp embedding. This can be frustrating when trying to pull images from the web into a personal project.

  5. SVG

    6. .svg files contain a slightly different type of image than the rest of these. Scalable Vector Graphics do not lose quality when scaled up or down, because they are not raster image files. They are created using geometry and math rather than pixels, allowing them to be high quality at all times. It is difficult to get organic looking colors with .svg files, so they are better suited for logomarks or cartoons as opposed to photography or painterly illustration.
