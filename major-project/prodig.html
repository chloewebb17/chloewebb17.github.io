Production I.G

History

Production I.G was created in the midst of an economic bubble. Mitsuhisa Ishikawa and Takayuki Hoto bounced of the back of the giant Tatsunoko Production (responsible for Speed Racer) and formed I.G Tatsunoko, which would later be given its current name. Production I.G was, right off the bat, exceptionally good at coordinating highly advanced projects with the best directors out there, such as the acclaimed Ghost in the Shell by Mamoru Oshii. The studio established its own holding company, which is unusual for anime studios (which are often funded by committees). Production I.G often negotiates partnerships with other studios in Japan and abroad, and as such has participated in projects like Evangelion, FLCL, Attack on Titan, and Quentin Tarantino's Kill Bill. They have also frequently collaborated with CLAMP. Production I.G formed WIT Studio below them to produce the highly acclaimed Attack on Titan. WIT is also a strong collaborator with other studios and produces very high-quality animation. Due to its business strengths, Production I.G is one of the few studios that remains completely financially independant, and represents what it means to run a creative business.

Specialty

I.G was a pioneer during the transition between traditional hand-drawn animation and digital animation. The process of creating animation digitally is very different than using paper, and many studios weren't prepared for the change. When producing the film Blood: The Last Vampire, the studio developed techniques that primarily pushed efficiency rather than disruption for the animators. They continued to allow animators to complete their rough animations on pencil and paper, which is largely faster than digital drawing due to the limitations in physical space, but expedited the inking and coloring process using digital programs. The fill-tool turned a multi-hour, highly technical cel painting process into a few clicks and touch-ups per frame. The Japanese government even funded the development of this technique.

Ghost in the Shell

Mamoru Oshii is a highly acclaimed Japanese filmmaker. He worked on Kerberos Panzer Cop and later its film adaptation Jin-Roh: the Wolf Brigade with Production I.G. He also directed Angel's Egg and most famously, Ghost in the Shell. Some who worked on Angel's Egg went on to found Studio Ghibli. What's most notable about Ghost in the Shell is that it was a massive hit, even in America, when anime was relatively unknown and unpopular aside from the occasional Ghibli film. Ghost in the Shell was so popular overseas that it attracted the attention of the Wachowskis, who credit it as a primary inspiration for The Matrix. Though Ghost in the Shell was based on a manga of the same name, the film went on to recieve multiple original sequels, TV series, web series, and a very bad live-action film starring Scarlett Johansson. Today, Ghost in the Shell is recieving a new anime series adapatation by Science SARU that is independant of the Production I.G franchise and instead takes its inspiration from the manga, but it remains clear that this new adaptation is only possible because of the raging success of Mamoru Oshii's film.
