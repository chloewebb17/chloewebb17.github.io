Gainax

Gainax was originally formed under the name "Daicon Film" by students from Osaka University of Arts including the legendary Hideaki Anno, who would go on to create his and the company's magnum opus. But before we get there, Daicon Film was hard at work making short films for science-fiction conventions, causing its members to neglect their schoolwork and drop out of college due to the enormous amount of effort they were putting in. After getting expelled from Osaka University of Arts, Hideaki Anno independantly went to work on the film Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind, which was the first film by the most celebrated anime director of all time, two-time Oscar winner Hayao Miyazaki. Those who worked on this film, besides Anno, went on to form Studio Ghibli, who I will not be talking about on this site because they are their own behemoth of a studio. After returning to Daicon Film, Anno and the others decided to rename themselves Gainax and begin working on larger projects.

Gainax always produced original projects, unlike most anime studios that adapt manga properties for TV. Some of Gainax's original projects became the most celebrated, influential anime of all time, such as Neon Genesis Evangelion. Gainax originals are known for their controversial twist endings, and Evangelion may be one of the best examples of that (though I won't spoil it here!). Some other interesting projects include FLCL, a 6-episode miniseries that was massively influential in America and is the,core inspiration for Avatar: The Last Airbender, and the absurdly raunchy Panty & Stocking with Garterbelt, which is intentionally styled to look like an Adult Swim or MTV cartoon from the early 2000's. In all of its projects, Gainax is known for mind-blowing, experimental action and style.
