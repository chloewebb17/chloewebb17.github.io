Madhouse

History

Madhouse was formed by ex-staff members of Mushi Production, which you will come to learn is the mother of all anime studios, responsible for 60's classics such as Astro Boy, Dororo, and Ashita no Joe. Mushi also worked on the Rankin/Bass animated Christmas specials like Frosty the Snowman. Madhouse has always had overall high quality anime, producing classics like Cardcaptor Sakura and Trigun, but they often go above and beyond, and have produced some of the best-looking anime movies ever made. In the 2000's, Madhouse produced all of Satoshi Kon's projects up until his death. Then, Madhouse set its sights on the ambitious Redline, a space-race action flick. The animation in Redline was incredibly complex, and the studio sunk more and more money into it as production dragged longer than anticipated. When all was said and done, Redline had over 100,000 hand-drawn frames (that's way more than usual) and was a total flop in theatres. Though it is considered a cult classic, the studio was on the verge of bankrupty. Thankfully, it was saved when Nippon TV acquired 95% of the company at the last second. Today, Madhouse still produces heavy hitters and is known for its stellar animation. Its current golden egg, Frieren: Beyond Journey's End holds the top spot on My Anime List, with over 882,000 good reviews.

Specialty

Gainax always produced original projects, unlike most anime studios that adapt manga properties for TV. Some of Gainax's original projects became the most celebrated, influential anime of all time, such as Neon Genesis Evangelion. Gainax originals are known for their controversial twist endings, and Evangelion may be one of the best examples of that (though I won't spoil it here!). Words can't really do Evangelion's impact justice, so just know that all anime can basically be divided into two eras: pre-and-post Eva. Some other interesting projects include FLCL, a bold, eclectic 6-episode miniseries that was massively influential in America and is the core inspiration for Avatar: The Last Airbender, and the absurdly raunchy Panty & Stocking with Garterbelt, which is intentionally styled to look like an Adult Swim or MTV cartoon from the early 2000's. In all of its projects, Gainax is known for mind-blowing experimental action and style.

Satoshi Kon

Gainax controversially mismanaged their money and was even convicted of tax evasion following Evangelion's success. Following this and many other incidents such as lawsuits, arrests, and an outright refusal to produce any anime projects as opposed to sitting on piles of cash, Gainax went bankrupt and dissolved in 2024. However, the core teams that made up Gainax in its glory days had already split off into two new, successful studios: Anno and Evangelion became Khara, and the rest split off after making Gurren Lagann to form Studio Trigger, the only major anime studio that still produces mostly original projects today (and in my opinion, one of the most stylish studios out there. Too bad they haven't made the cut for this website just yet.)
