Madhouse

History

Madhouse was formed by ex-staff members of Mushi Production, which you will come to learn is the mother of all anime studios, responsible for 60's classics such as Astro Boy, Dororo, and Ashita no Joe. Mushi also worked on the Rankin/Bass animated Christmas specials like Frosty the Snowman. Since its inception in the 1970's, Madhouse has had a reputation for high-quality productions, responsible for classics like Cardcaptor Sakura and Trigun, but they often go above and beyond, and have produced some of the best-looking anime movies ever made. In the 2000's, Madhouse produced all of Satoshi Kon's projects up until his death. Then, Madhouse set its sights on the ambitious Redline, a space-race action flick. The animation in Redline was incredibly complex, and the studio sunk more and more money into it as production dragged longer than anticipated. When all was said and done, Redline had over 100,000 hand-drawn frames (that's way more than usual) and was a total flop in theatres. Though it is considered a cult classic, the studio was on the verge of bankrupty. Thankfully, it was saved when Nippon TV acquired 95% of the company at the last second. Today, Madhouse still produces heavy hitters and is known for its stellar animation. Its current golden egg, Frieren: Beyond Journey's End holds the top spot on My Anime List, with over 882,000 good reviews. Personally, I like Frieren, but it doesn't quite deserve this spot- not next to Madhouse's other amazing projects.

Specialty

Madhouse is known for its exceptionally high quality animation. While many other studios struggled massively to uphold their strengths when anime transitioned from hand-painted cels to digital drawings, Madhouse reinforced their fundamental strengths in character drawing, layout art, and color design. Bit of a soapbox moment here, but color design in anime is substantially worse in the modern day for reasons that mystify me- Madhouse productions often being the exception. Madhouse is very good at producing films, such as Redline, but also Mamoru Hosoda's movies, The Girl Who Leapt Through Time and Summer Wars. After these two films were complete, the acclaimed director split off from Madhouse with Yuichiro Saito to form Studio Chizu.

Satoshi Kon

Gainax controversially mismanaged their money and was even convicted of tax evasion following Evangelion's success. Following this and many other incidents such as lawsuits, arrests, and an outright refusal to produce any anime projects as opposed to sitting on piles of cash, Gainax went bankrupt and dissolved in 2024. However, the core teams that made up Gainax in its glory days had already split off into two new, successful studios: Anno and Evangelion became Khara, and the rest split off after making Gurren Lagann to form Studio Trigger, the only major anime studio that still produces mostly original projects today (and in my opinion, one of the most stylish studios out there. Too bad they haven't made the cut for this website just yet.)
