Madhouse

History

Madhouse was formed by ex-staff members of Mushi Production, which you will come to learn is the mother of all anime studios, responsible for 60's classics such as Astro Boy, Dororo, and Ashita no Joe. Mushi also worked on the Rankin/Bass animated Christmas specials like Frosty the Snowman. Since its inception in the 1970's, Madhouse has had a reputation for high-quality productions, responsible for classics like Cardcaptor Sakura and Trigun, but they often go above and beyond, and have produced some of the best-looking anime movies ever made. In the 2000's, Madhouse produced all of Satoshi Kon's projects up until his death. Then, Madhouse set its sights on the ambitious Redline, a space-race action flick. The animation in Redline was incredibly complex, and the studio sunk more and more money into it as production dragged longer than anticipated. When all was said and done, Redline had over 100,000 hand-drawn frames (that's way more than usual) and was a total flop in theatres. Though it is considered a cult classic, the studio was on the verge of bankrupty. Thankfully, it was saved when Nippon TV acquired 95% of the company at the last second. Today, Madhouse still produces heavy hitters and is known for its stellar animation. Its current golden egg, Frieren: Beyond Journey's End holds the top spot on My Anime List, with over 882,000 good reviews. Personally, I like Frieren, but it doesn't quite deserve this spot- not next to Madhouse's other amazing projects.

Specialty

Madhouse is known for its exceptionally high quality animation. While many other studios struggled massively to uphold their strengths when anime transitioned from hand-painted cels to digital drawings, Madhouse reinforced their fundamental strengths in character drawing, layout art, and color design. Bit of a soapbox moment here, but color design in anime is substantially worse in the modern day for reasons that mystify me- Madhouse productions often being the exception. Madhouse is very good at producing films, such as Redline, but also Mamoru Hosoda's movies, The Girl Who Leapt Through Time and Summer Wars. Satoshi Kon

Satoshi Kon was an anime director and screenwriter. He only directed 5 projects in his life: Perfect Blue, Millenium Actress, Tokyo Godfathers, Paranoia Agent, and Paprika. The last of which was debatably ripped off as a base for the movie Inception- though Christopher Nolan denies this. Black Swan is also sometimes called a Perfect Blue ripoff, but that comparison appears to be more about shot composition than actual plot beats. It can't be overstated how incredible each of Kon's films are. Kon was heavily influenced by live action filmmaking, and as such, his projects had realistic, weighty character animation, nuanced depictions of life in Japan, and extraordinarily good design. Kon sadly lost his battle against pancreatic cancer in 2010 ate age 46, and his final project was never finished. In my opinion, it is quite possible that Satoshi Kon was the greatest anime director to ever live.
