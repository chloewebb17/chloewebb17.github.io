Kyoto Animation

History

Kyoto Animation (often referred to as KyoAni) was co-founded by a married couple, Yoko and Hideaki Hatta, along with many ex-staff members from Mushi Production. It is also a light novel publisher. Yoko had studied directly under the father of manga and anime, Osamu Tezuka. At first, the company was a limited group that assisted with other projects, but they opened their own practice up and became a corporation after a few years. Like their name suggests, they are headquartered in the city of Kyoto. KyoAni specializes in slice of life subjects, feminine aesthetics, and did especially well during the moe-boom of the 2000's. This is sometimes called the "Cute Girls Doing Cute Things" genre. Frankly, KyoAni is the very best at this.

Specialty

KyoAni, being founded by a woman, employs more women on average than most anime studios. In fact, anime production is very much a men's club. This creates a diverse perspective when it comes to the in-house creative process. Another key difference that makes KyoAni so special is that its employees are salaried workers. In the case of almost all other studios, animators work on a per-project basis. They may still be full-time employees, but they only get paid for the projects they work on. In an industry where being overworked and underpaid is the norm, KyoAni employees are reportedly treated ethically. Being on a salary allows employees to destress between projects and focus on honing their creativity. Not only that, but KyoAni puts in the extra effort to train their employees. Many other studios expect their employees to have the skills they need when they apply for their jobs, but KyoAni offers extensive training to bring employees up to standard and allows them room to grow. They also never hire freelancers, whose work would undercut the rest of the employees.

2019 Arson Attack

It breaks my heart to end off this article with this, but it would be dishonest of me not to. In 2019, a man entered Kyoto Animation's building on a work day and doused the place and many people with gasoline. Witnesses say they heard him talking about plagiarism before he lit a fire. He and many others caught on fire and were seen fleeing the building, but many others were trapped inside. There were also many reports of an explosion coming from inside the building at the time. The man was chased down and apprehended, but of the 70 employeees who were in the building, 36 passed away and 34 were injured, making it one of the deadliest arson attacks ever. The arsonist pleaded insanity, but was sentenced to death in 2024. The attack on Kyoto Animation was, and still is, unthinkable. Not only did a significant portion of the studio perish in the attack, but allmost all of their work, past and present, was destroyed. Kyoto Animation responded to this tragedy with astonishing resilience, opting to push on and open up its training program to more young animators. The attack had a deep impact on the entire animation industry. Of particular note, Look Back is a movie based on a manga one-shot by Tatsuki Fujimoto that is metaphorical for how the attack impacted him and his desire to make art. It is highly worth a watch.
